Modified kodo millet bran serves as a prebiotic fiber, selectively nourishing beneficial gut bacteria and boosting short-chain fatty acid (SCFA) production—particularly butyrate. SCFAs are known to inhibit neuroinflammation, improve mitochondrial function, and stabilize neuronal excitability. By combining this prebiotic with the psychobiotic LAB6, the researchers aimed to create a synergistic effect: the fiber reshapes the microbial community to favor GABA and SCFA producers, while the bacterium directly supplies neuroactive GABA and additional anti-inflammatory metabolites.
