The review underscores that disruptions in the gut microbiome can amplify liver damage, creating a feedback loop that promotes fibrosis and potentially cancer. This microbial-immune-metabolic network offers new insights into liver disease progression and potential therapeutic targets.

By integrating microbiome research with oncology, these strategies aim to personalize cancer care and reduce resistance. This work underscores the growing importance of the microbiota in shaping cancer therapy and offers promising directions for future research and clinical applications.

A new systematic review in Sleep Medicine Reviews has mapped how sleep and circadian rhythms shape the gut microbiome across the human lifespan. Analyzing 41 studies from 2016-2025, researchers found consistent links between disrupted sleep patterns, irregular circadian timing, and altered gut microbial composition in generally healthy participants. The review highlights that both experimental and observational studies point to bidirectional relationships—poor sleep can shift microbiome diversity, while gut microbes may influence sleep quality. These findings underscore the importance of sleep hygiene and circadian alignment not just for rest, but for maintaining a healthy gut ecosystem. The authors call for more longitudinal research to clarify causality and explore therapeutic potential for sleep-based microbiome interventions.

The development of such precision tools for manipulating the tumor microbiome opens new avenues for cancer therapy that complement traditional treatments.

A new study in Environmental Research reveals that mobile genetic elements are key drivers in assembling high-risk combinations of antibiotic resistance and virulence genes at the water-sediment interface in rivers. By integrating shotgun metagenomics with pollutant profiling along a rural-to-urban gradient, the research shows how microplastics, heavy metals, and antibiotics co-occur to shape resistance and virulence risks in riverine ecosystems. The findings highlight the critical role of mobile genetic elements in facilitating the spread of dangerous microbial traits in these environments. This work underscores the need to consider multiple pollutants and their interactions when assessing environmental health risks in aquatic systems.

A new study in Cancer Research Communications links Clostridioides difficile test positivity to a higher incidence of colorectal cancer. Researchers analyzed hospital-based retrospective cohort data and found that patients with positive C. difficile tests had an elevated risk of developing sporadic colorectal cancer. The findings build on growing evidence that gut microbiome disruptions—particularly colonization by certain bacteria—may influence CRC development. While prior work in mouse models suggested prolonged C. difficile exposure could promote tumor formation, this human data strengthens the association. The authors note that environmental and microbial factors are increasingly recognized as contributors to CRC beyond traditional risk factors. They call for further investigation into whether C. difficile infection or its associated dysbiosis plays a causal role in colorectal carcinogenesis.

A new review in Comprehensive Physiology highlights the emerging "gut-liver-lung" axis as a key player in pulmonary vascular diseases. Researchers explain that while the liver and lungs normally work in close harmony, disruptions in this relationship—especially involving the gut microbiome—can drive disease progression. The study emphasizes that changes in gut bacteria can influence liver function, which in turn affects lung blood vessels, potentially worsening conditions like pulmonary hypertension. This interconnected system offers new insights into disease mechanisms and could open doors for innovative therapies targeting the microbiome or liver to improve lung health. The findings underscore the importance of viewing these organs as an integrated network rather than in isolation.

Berries are emerging as powerful allies for gut health, according to a review in Nutrition Reviews. Rich in polyphenols, dietary fiber, and micronutrients, berries exhibit antioxidant, anti-inflammatory, and microbiota-modulating effects. The review explores how berry consumption influences gut health across four domains: microbiota composition and function, intestinal barrier integrity, immune modulation, and protection against gastrointestinal diseases.
